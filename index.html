Linkin Park History

Linkin Park is an American rock band formed in 1996 in Agoura Hills, California, originally under the name Xero. The lineup included Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, and Rob Bourdon, later joined by Joe Hahn, Dave "Phoenix" Farrell, and Chester Bennington. The band skyrocketed to fame with their debut album *Hybrid Theory* (2000), which went diamond in the U.S. Their unique style—blending nu-metal, alternative rock, and electronic music—defined the sound of a generation.

With hits like *In the End*, *Numb*, and *Crawling*, Linkin Park became the voice of the 2000s youth. They experimented with genres, from the heavy metal of *Meteora* (2003) to the cinematic soundscapes of *A Thousand Suns* (2010). Despite the tragic loss of Chester in 2017, their music continues to inspire millions worldwide.

Chester Bennington: The Soul of the Band

Chester Bennington joined Linkin Park in 1999, becoming its iconic vocalist. His powerful voice and emotional depth breathed life into lyrics about struggle, pain, and hope. Born on March 20, 1976, in Phoenix, Arizona, Chester battled personal demons, which echoed through the band’s work.

His passing on July 20, 2017, shook the world, but Chester’s legacy lives on in every song. From *Breaking the Habit* to *One More Light*, his vocals remain a timeless symbol of strength and vulnerability.

Fun Facts

  • The name "Linkin Park" is a play on "Lincoln Park" in Santa Monica, tweaked due to a taken domain.
  • *Hybrid Theory* was originally planned as Mike Shinoda’s solo project.
  • Chester recorded the vocals for *Numb* in a single take!
  • The band collaborated with Jay-Z, creating the iconic *Collision Course* (2004).
